Architectural Archetypes

Eight ways a body
inhabits space.

Each archetype is a precise classification of spatial preference — derived from physiological response patterns, not aesthetic opinion. Your archetype is not chosen. It is measured.

08

archetypes
01 — Naturalist
The Naturalist
Physiologically drawn to biophilic environments — spaces that mediate the boundary between interior and natural world. Strong GSR response to organic materials and daylighting.
Biophilic · Organic
02 — Explorer
The Explorer
Elevated arousal response to spatial complexity and thresholds. Gaze trajectories indicate active navigation behaviour — seeks layered depth and prospect over refuge.
Spatial Complexity
03 — Sanctuary Seeker
The Sanctuary Seeker
GSR stabilisation in enclosed, acoustically isolated environments. The parasympathetic response to protective spatial enclosure.
Refuge · Enclosure
04 — Social Showcaser
The Social Showcaser
Positive biometric response to spaces that facilitate observation and social performance. Pupil dilation increases with spatial grandeur.
Social · Performative
05 — Futurist
The Futurist
Sustained attention to parametric geometries and material innovation. Gaze lingers at structural interfaces — drawn to spaces that signal technological mastery.
Parametric · Technological
06 — Traditionalist
The Traditionalist
Physiological comfort response to historical material palettes, proportion systems, and classical spatial hierarchies.
Classical · Proportional
07 — Cocooner
The Cocooner
Strong biometric preference for intimate scale, textured softness, and low luminance. Parasympathetic activation in domestically scaled environments.
Intimate · Sensory
08 — Minimal Haven
The Minimal Haven
Cognitive clarity response to spatial reduction. Elimination of visual noise induces measurable calm. Gaze patterns become long and restful in uncluttered volumes.
Reductive · Cognitive

The Session

From stranger to understood
in fifteen minutes.

A single, structured session replaces the open-ended back-and-forth of conventional briefing. The client experiences eight architectural environments in VR. The body responds without instruction. You receive a precise physiological brief.

0:00
Setup
Client is fitted with GSR sensors and eye-tracking hardware. Baseline biometric readings are established over 60 seconds of rest.
~2 min
2:00
VR Walkthrough
Client navigates 8 VR architectural scenes. Gaze, GSR, and pupil dilation are recorded continuously. No instruction is given — response is entirely involuntary.
~5 min
7:00
Questionnaire
A structured 12-question self-report instrument captures conscious spatial preference. Responses are weighted at 0.5× against biometric evidence.
5–10 min
15:00
Archetype Report
Primary and secondary archetypes are calculated. A complete physiological brief is generated — ready for immediate use in design.
